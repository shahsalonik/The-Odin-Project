Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti, one of the most beloved and iconic pasta varieties, has its origins deeply rooted in Italian cuisine. This long, thin, cylindrical pasta is traditionally made from durum wheat semolina, which gives it a firm texture and a golden hue. Spaghetti is incredibly versatile and forms the basis of numerous classic dishes, ranging from the simple yet delightful spaghetti aglio e olio, made with garlic and olive oil, to the robust and hearty spaghetti Bolognese, featuring a rich meat sauce. In Italy, the preparation of spaghetti is considered an art, with precise attention given to the pasta's cooking time to achieve the perfect al dente texture, where it is tender yet firm to the bite.

Beyond its Italian heritage, spaghetti has become a global staple, celebrated in kitchens around the world for its adaptability and ease of cooking. It pairs well with a vast array of sauces and ingredients, making it a go-to choice for quick weeknight dinners and elaborate culinary creations alike. Spaghetti's ability to complement both simple and complex flavors has led to innovative dishes, including variations like spaghetti carbonara with its creamy egg-based sauce and spaghetti marinara loaded with seafood. As a symbol of comfort food, spaghetti not only nourishes the body but also evokes a sense of home and tradition, bridging cultures and generations through its enduring appeal.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Olive oil
  • Spaghetti
  • Marinara sauce
  • Chili flakes
  • Basil
  • Oregano
  • Garlic
  • Parmesan

Steps

  1. Bring the water to a boil. Put the spaghetti in there and cook until al-dente.
  2. Drain the water and spaghetti using a colander.
  3. Pour some olive oil into the same pot and put the herbs and garlic into the oil when it starts getting shiny.
  4. Put the spaghetti into the pot and stir well, ensuring the herbs and garlic are incorporated well.
  5. Pour in the marinara sauce and stir well.
  6. Serve while hot and top with parmesan if needed.
